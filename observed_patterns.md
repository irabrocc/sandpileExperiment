The "thickness" of a line depends solely on its angle of inclination.
Perfectly horizontal or vertical lines are the thinnest, only 1 grid cell wide, and the entire line is solid green.
Lines with a slight tilt (e.g., moving 1 grid cell upward for every 2 grid cell to the right) are thicker.
The steeper the tilt of the line, the thicker it becomes.

For lines of the same angle of inclination, their thickness is always constant—no part of the line is thicker or thinner than another.
Along the entire length of any line, the pattern of colors repeats exactly without variation.
For example, a 3-grid-cell-wide line will always follow the "green-yellow-green" sequence from left to right, maintaining this exact order along its full length. 

The closer a line’s slope is to horizontal or vertical, the less frequently it appears in a single trial. Among the observed trials, most lines are horizontal, vertical or oriented at a 45-degree angle, while lines with slopes of (1, 2) or (1, 3) are rare.

The sum of the unit direction vectors of all lines meeting at a single intersection point equals zero.
